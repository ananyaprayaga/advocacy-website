Kinder farms for a kinder planet.

What is agricultural animal abuse?

Agricultural animal abuse is a widespread issue rooted in modern factory farming practices, where animals are often treated as mere production units. Billions of animals, including cows, pigs, and chickens, endure cramped living conditions, limited or no access to sunlight, and unnatural diets designed to maximize growth and profits. These animals are frequently subjected to painful procedures such as tail docking, debeaking, or castration without anesthesia. Many live their entire lives in tiny cages or crowded facilities, unable to express natural behaviors like grazing or nesting. Such treatment causes immense physical and psychological suffering, making it clear that the welfare of these animals is often neglected for efficiency and cost-effectiveness.

Our mission

Our mission is to raise awareness of this issue and encourage likeminded people to take action, whether it be through simply signing our petition or making a donation, or even just engaging with other organizations that share the same goal as ours.
